## Research Summary

Postbiotics represent the next generation of microbiome-friendly cosmetic ingredients. Unlike probiotics (live bacteria) or prebiotics (food for bacteria), postbiotics are the beneficial metabolites, cell wall fragments, enzymes, and bioactive compounds produced by probiotic bacteria. They offer stability advantages over live cultures while delivering skin microbiome benefits including barrier strengthening, anti-inflammatory effects, and enhanced skin health. This emerging category is gaining significant traction in the clean beauty and science-backed skincare markets.

---

## What Are Postbiotics?

**Definition:** Non-viable bacterial products or metabolic byproducts from probiotic microorganisms that have biologically activity in the host.

**Types of Postbiotics:**
- Bacterial lysates (cell fragments)
- Fermentation metabolites
- Cell-free supernatants
- Exopolysaccharides
- Short-chain fatty acids
- Peptides and proteins
- Organic acids

**Benefits for Skin:**
- Skin barrier reinforcement
- Anti-inflammatory properties
- Microbiome balance support
- Enhanced moisture retention
- Improved skin resilience
- Antimicrobial activity
- Soothing and calming effects
