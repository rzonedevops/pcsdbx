## Sterilization Methods Comparison

### Steam Sterilization (Autoclaves)
**Best For:** Metal tools, glassware, some plastics, water-based solutions
**Advantages:** Effective, economical, fast, widely accepted
**Disadvantages:** Not suitable for heat-sensitive materials
**Cost:** $2,000-$100,000+ depending on size and features

### Dry Heat Sterilization
**Best For:** Oils, powders, metal items, glassware
**Advantages:** No moisture, no corrosion, penetrates closed containers
**Disadvantages:** Longer cycles, higher temperatures, not for plastics
**Cost:** $1,500-$15,000

### UV Sterilization
**Best For:** Surface sterilization, air, clean rooms, packaging materials
**Advantages:** Chemical-free, fast, no heat, low operating cost
**Disadvantages:** Surface only (no penetration), requires direct exposure
**Cost:** $500-$10,000

### Hydrogen Peroxide (VHP)
**Best For:** Heat-sensitive items, clean rooms, isolators, large spaces
**Advantages:** Low temperature, effective, no toxic residue
**Disadvantages:** Expensive equipment, requires training, longer cycles
**Cost:** $20,000-$100,000+

## Regulatory Considerations

### FDA Requirements (USA)
- GMP compliance requires validated sterilization procedures
- Documentation of sterilization cycles
- Regular biological indicator testing
- Preventive maintenance records
- Qualification/validation of equipment (IQ/OQ/PQ)

### EU Requirements
- CE marking required for sterilization equipment
- Compliance with EN standards (EN 13060 for small steam sterilizers)
- Validation documentation required
- Regular performance verification

### ISO Standards
- **ISO 17665:** Sterilization of health care products - Moist heat
- **ISO 13485:** Medical devices quality management
- **ISO 14937:** General requirements for characterization of sterilizing agents
