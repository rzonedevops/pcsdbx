## Key UV Filter Types

### Organic (Chemical) UV Filters
Organic UV filters are carbon-based molecules that absorb UV radiation and convert it into harmless heat. They provide approximately 90% of their protective effect through absorption, with 10% from scattering. Common organic filters include avobenzone, octinoxate, octocrylene, and homosalate.

### Inorganic (Mineral) UV Filters
Inorganic UV filters are mineral-based, primarily zinc oxide (ZnO) and titanium dioxide (TiO2). They are produced as crystalline particles that absorb UV radiation (90% of protective effect) and scatter/reflect light (10% of protective effect). These filters require surface coatings to prevent photocatalytic activity and free radical formation.

### Particulate Organic UV Filters
Some organic filters are prepared as particulate dispersions due to low solubility in cosmetic oils or water. When particle size is less than 100 nm, they are considered nanoparticles. These provide broad-spectrum protection through both absorption and scattering.

## Technical Insights from BASF Research

### UV Filter Mechanisms
- **Absorption:** Primary mechanism (90% of protection) - UV radiation is absorbed and converted to harmless heat
- **Scattering:** Secondary mechanism (10% of protection) - UV rays are scattered away from skin
- **Reflection:** Minor contribution to overall protection

### Formulation Considerations
- Maximum concentration limits: Inorganic oxides (25%), organic filters (4-15%)
- Sensory technology is critical for consumer acceptance
- UV filters must work in sync with emulsifiers, oils, and other ingredients
- Photostability is essential to maintain protection over time
- Surface coatings on inorganic filters prevent photocatalytic activity

### Modern UV Protection Requirements
- **Broad-spectrum protection:** UVB + UVA + (increasingly) blue light
- **Health pillar:** Reduce skin cancer risk
- **Cosmetic pillar:** Prevent premature aging, wrinkles, and photoaging
- **Sensory appeal:** Products must feel pleasant for regular use
