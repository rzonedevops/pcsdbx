## Hair Care Actives — New Listings (April 2026)

### 1. Provital Group (Spain)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Hair_Care_Actives/provital_group_hair_actives.json`
- **HQ:** Barcelona, Spain
- **Website:** www.provitalgroup.com
- **Key Products:** Baicapil™ (Scutellaria baicalensis + wheat sprout + soy sprout tri-complex), ProAmplex®, Exostem Hair
- **Rationale:** Baicapil™ is one of the most clinically cited plant-based anti-hair loss actives in formulation guides and ingredient databases. Provital is vertically integrated — they grow and extract their botanicals — giving them excellent Ecocert/COSMOS credentials.
- **Clinical Data:** Baicapil™ — 22% increase in anagen hair, 57% reduction in hair loss vs. placebo
- **Certifications:** Ecocert, COSMOS, NATRUE, ISO 9001

### 2. Lipotec — Lubrizol Life Science Beauty (Spain)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Hair_Care_Actives/lipotec_hair_peptides.json`
- **HQ:** Barcelona, Spain (part of Lubrizol / Berkshire Hathaway)
- **Website:** www.lipotec.com
- **Key Products:** Biotinoyl Tripeptide-1, Acetyl Tetrapeptide-3, Vividine®
- **Rationale:** Lipotec is the originator of the most widely referenced synthetic hair growth peptides used in premium anti-hair loss formulations globally. Biotinoyl Tripeptide-1 is a key component of the commercial Procapil® ingredient used by dozens of hair care brands.
- **Note:** Lubrizol already appears in hair care actives as a texture/conditioning supplier; this listing specifically covers Lipotec's peptide synthesis for hair growth applications, which is distinct.

### 3. TRI-K Industries (USA)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Hair_Care_Actives/tri_k_industries_hair.json`
- **HQ:** Northvale, New Jersey, USA
- **Website:** www.tri-k.com
- **Key Products:** Keratein™, Kera-Quat™, Croquat® series, quinoa proteins
- **Rationale:** TRI-K is the leading specialist US supplier of hydrolysed protein actives for hair care. Their keratin hydrolysate chemistry is critical for premium hair repair formulations (bond repair, post-chemical-treatment recovery). Often cited alongside Croda and Ashland in professional haircare innovation.
- **Certifications:** ISO 9001, Kosher, Halal; vegan-certified options available

### 4. Symrise (Germany)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Hair_Care_Actives/symrise_hair_actives.json`
- **HQ:** Holzminden, Germany
- **Website:** www.symrise.com
- **Key Products:** SymHair™ series, SymSitive® scalp-calming active, bioferment hair actives, plant stem cell extracts
- **Rationale:** Symrise is one of the Big 4 global fragrance/cosmetic ingredient companies and is actively building out its hair care actives portfolio via its Diana Food and Cosmo International divisions. Their SymSitive® for sensitive scalp is gaining traction in premium scalp care. Adding Symrise brings important large-supplier balance to the Hair Care Actives category.

## Biotech Ingredients — New Listings (April 2026)

### 1. Amyris (USA)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Biotech_Ingredients/amyris_biotech.json`
- **HQ:** Emeryville, California, USA
- **Website:** www.amyris.com
- **Key Products:** Neossance® Squalane, Neossance® Hemisqualane, Purecane™ CBG, β-Farnesene platform
- **Rationale:** Amyris is the most commercially successful precision fermentation company in personal care ingredients. Their Neossance® Squalane is the global gold standard for bio-based squalane with Ecocert/COSMOS certification, used by hundreds of prestige brands. This is a foundational biotech supplier that should have been in the database earlier.
- **Technology:** Engineered yeast fermenting sugarcane sucrose → farnesene → squalane/hemisqualane

### 2. Ginkgo Bioworks (USA)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Biotech_Ingredients/ginkgo_bioworks_cosmetics.json`
- **HQ:** Boston, Massachusetts, USA
- **Website:** www.ginkgobioworks.com
- **Key Products:** Organism engineering platform, fragrance biosynthesis, custom enzyme development, partnership model
- **Rationale:** Ginkgo is the infrastructure layer of synthetic biology — they enable other companies to produce biotech cosmetic actives at scale. Their partnerships with Robertet, Givaudan, and others are already shaping the next generation of biosynthetic fragrance and cosmetic actives. Critical strategic inclusion for completeness.

### 3. Debut Biotech (USA)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Biotech_Ingredients/debut_biotech_ingredients.json`
- **HQ:** San Diego, California, USA
- **Website:** www.debutbiotech.com
- **Key Products:** Cell-free biomanufacturing platform, ceramide biosynthesis, retinol precursor production
- **Rationale:** Debut represents a genuinely novel manufacturing paradigm — cell-free enzyme cascades that bypass the limitations of whole-cell fermentation. Their ceramide and retinoid precursor capabilities are particularly relevant to the SKIN-TWIN active ingredient recommendations engine.

### 4. Genomatica (USA)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Biotech_Ingredients/genomatica_biotech_ingredients.json`
- **HQ:** San Diego, California, USA
- **Website:** www.genomatica.com
- **Key Products:** Brontide™ Bio-BDO, bio-based chemical intermediates
- **Rationale:** Genomatica's bio-BDO is a strategically important cosmetic ingredient (used as humectant, solvent, and preservative booster). The USDA BioPreferred certification and commercial-scale supply partnership with Novamont makes this a credible large-volume biotech ingredient supplier.

### 5. Algenics (France)
- **File:** `listings/Raw_Materials/Biotech_Ingredients/algenics_microalgae_biotech.json`
- **HQ:** Saint-Nazaire, France
- **Website:** www.algenics.com
- **Key Products:** Algaktiv® Firm+, Algaktiv® Nutri+, diatom exopolysaccharides
- **Rationale:** Algenics fills an important gap in microalgae biotechnology — specifically closed-system photobioreactor cultivation of diatoms for cosmetic-grade polysaccharides. Their Algaktiv® Firm+ has clinical data for skin firmness, making this a strong addition alongside BASF and Givaudan in the biotech actives segment.

### 7. BASF Personal Care — Biotech Division
