## Executive Summary

Texture modifiers, film formers, and sensory enhancers are critical functional ingredients that define the consumer experience of skincare and cosmetic products. This category encompasses a wide range of materials — from silicone alternatives and film formers to mattifying powders and soft-focus agents. The global market for cosmetic sensory modifiers is growing rapidly as consumers increasingly demand both performance and elegant skin feel.

This is a **zero-listing category** in pcsdbx and represents a significant gap for the SKIN-TWIN formulation engine, which will need to recommend optimal texture systems for formulations.

Key subcategories include:
1. **Film Formers** — Long-wear, barrier, and protective films
2. **Mattifying Agents** — Oil absorption and shine control
3. **Soft-Focus Powders** — Light diffusion and line-filling
4. **Sensory Modifiers** — Skin feel, slip, and texture enhancement
5. **Silicone Alternatives** — Sustainable, bio-based sensory ingredients
